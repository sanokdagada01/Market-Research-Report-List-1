<p><p>CRO, or Contract Research Organization, plays a crucial role in clinical trials, facilitating various phases of drug development. In Phase I trials, CROs focus on safety and dosage in healthy volunteers. Phase II trials assess efficacy and side effects in a larger patient group. Phase III trials involve extensive testing for effectiveness and monitor adverse reactions in diverse populations before regulatory approval. Phase IV trials, conducted post-approval, gather long-term data on treatment effects and further assess safety in the general population.</p></p>
