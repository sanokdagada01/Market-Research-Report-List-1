<p><p>The pediatric asthma treatment market is categorized into long-term control medications, quick-relief medications, and others. Long-term control medications, such as inhaled corticosteroids, are used daily to manage chronic symptoms and reduce airway inflammation. Quick-relief medications, like bronchodilators, provide immediate relief during asthma attacks. The "others" category includes biologics and combination therapies, which target specific asthma triggers or severity levels. Together, these categories ensure comprehensive asthma management for children, aiming to improve their quality of life and prevent exacerbations.</p></p>
