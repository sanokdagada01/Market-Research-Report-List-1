<p><p>The automotive proximity sensor market is primarily divided into analog and digital sensors. Analog sensors provide continuous voltage output, detecting the presence of nearby objects based on varying distances, resulting in smooth range detection. Conversely, digital sensors offer discrete signals, indicating whether an object is within a set range or not, which ensures precise on/off detection. Both types are used for applications like parking assistance and collision avoidance, enhancing vehicle safety and driving convenience.</p></p>
